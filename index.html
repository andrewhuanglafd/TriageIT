Overview

This manual covers the Simple Triage And Rapid Treatment algorithm — the standard sorting method for adult mass-casualty incidents. Sections 01–03 cover the doctrine. Sections 04–05 describe how to use this app to drill it. Section 06 is the disclaimer; read it.

01

The Job

Read each patient. Run the algorithm in order. Pick a tag. Move on. The first arriving responders set tags so resources flow to the patients who need them most.

KEY DOCTRINE
Sort, don't treat. The only on-tag interventions allowed in START are airway repositioning and direct-pressure hemorrhage control. Everything else waits for transport.
02

YELLOW
Delayed. Significant injury, can wait.

BLACK

03

START Algorithm

Three vitals after the walk check: Respirations, Perfusion, Mental status. Take them in order. Stop at the first criterion that triggers a tag.

04

Drill Modes

TRAINING

Calm self-paced practice. One patient at a time, no clock, no penalties. Tap a tag, see the verdict, read the rationale. Pick a deck size — Sprint, Standard, Long, Marathon, or any custom number. Letter grade and rank at the end. Review every miss.

GAME MODE

3-minute MCI scene. Multiple patients on the board at once, each deteriorating in real time. Untreated yellows convert to reds. Untreated reds die on your watch. New patients arrive in waves. Score for speed and accuracy; lose points for over-triage and missed reds. Top mistakes flagged in the after-action report.

Correct RED+10
Correct YELLOW / GREEN / BLACK+5
Speed bonus (within 12 s)+5
Wrong tag−10
RED left to deteriorate to BLACK−15
MULTIPLAYER

Host shares a code; players join from their phones. Same cards, separate timers. Head-to-head or team-vs-team. Most correct wins; time breaks ties. Top 3 take gold, silver, bronze.

CLASSROOM

For instructors. Host a drill from your laptop, students join from their phones with a lobby code. You watch a live dashboard of every player's progress without playing yourself. Optional per-card decision timer. After the round, get a debrief showing which cards the crew missed most — tap any to expand the rationale and review as a group. Up to 50 students per lobby.

05

Difficulty Toggles

Two independent toggles available in Training and Game Mode. Pick one, the other, or both.

⚠ HARD

Vitals parked at the algorithm threshold. RR 31 is RED, but it doesn't look RED at a glance. Cap refill 3 s. Yellow patients at RR 30 that are tempting to call RED but aren't. Same algorithm — much tighter calls. No greens in the deck.

☠ EXPERT

Narratives become red herrings. Scary-looking walking-wounded who are still GREEN per START (ambulatory always wins). Calm-looking patients with hidden tachypnea. Patients who look DOA but have a pulse. Forces algorithm-driven decisions instead of gestalt. Sudden death: one wrong tag ends the run.

⚠ + ☠ HARD + EXPERT

Both stacked. Borderline vitals AND red-herring narratives AND sudden death. Maximum difficulty. The deck this produces is what a competent training officer would use to identify weak spots in their crew.

06

Doctrine & Disclaimer

TRAINING TOOL · NOT A PROTOCOL

A drill cannot replace certified MCI instruction. Real-world triage involves clinical judgment, scope of practice, on-scene context, and incomplete information beyond what a card can capture. Use this app as one piece of your training; it is not a substitute for your agency's standing orders or for in-person instruction.
